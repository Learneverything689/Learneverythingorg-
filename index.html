GRAPHOLOGY

Graphology is a study that analyzes handwriting in order to gain insight into the writer's personality traits, emotions, and even aptitudes. By examining the size, slant, spacing, and pressure of the writing, graphologists can make interpretations about the individual's characteristics and behaviors. While graphology is not considered a scientifically proven method of personality assessment, it is often used in forensic analysis, relationships counseling, and even employee screening processes. The practice of graphology dates back centuries and continues to fascinate many people who believe in its potential to reveal hidden aspects of a person's psyche through their handwriting. .
